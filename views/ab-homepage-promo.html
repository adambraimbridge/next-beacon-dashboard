A/B - Homepage follow promotion

Hypothesis

Following topics on Next has a positive outcome on frequency and recency of visits. Placing a prominent promotion of 'follow' at the top of the homepage increases the number of people following topics.

Summary

  • Over a 4 week period 326 people users followed at least one topic from the frontpage in the control, compared to 890 in the variant.
  • Putting something at the top of the frontpage does increase interactions.
  • Similar to a supermarket placing milk and bread at the back of the shop, we might say this space in general can be succesfully used to promote features that the user needs to walk (scroll) past in order to view the top stories.

Method

Users were segmented in to two groups. Both variants contained a promotion of following on the frontpage. Users in the control group saw it in the centre of the page, underneath the top stories. Users in the variant group saw it placed above the top stories.

Caveats

  • This was the first AB test written using the framework and we found a few problems. For four days in the middle of the test users in the variant group saw the control group variant.
